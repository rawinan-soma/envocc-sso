EnvOcc SSO — Login Design Directions

Four distinct visual personalities for the staff sign-in experience of the Division of Occupational and Environmental Diseases. All sit inside a “corporate, calm, trustworthy” family but differ in density, type, colour register, shape language and mood. Each shows the primary Sign-in hero, the secondary verification-code (MFA) screen, and one visible state variant (lockout or reset confirmation).

No real brand exists — text/abstract marks only. Voice: plain language, anti-phishing guidance first-class. Screens shown: Sign in · Verification code · State variant.
Direction 01 · Institutional

Civic Register

An official-notice register: serif wordmark on a navy rule, deep navy and a single restrained gold accent. The structure feels like government correspondence — measured, legitimate, and unmistakably sanctioned. Gold is used sparingly so authority never tips into decoration.

Direction 02 · Clinical

Clinic Light

A healthcare-portal calm: abundant white, a clear teal-blue accent and thin rules. Soft circular marks and generous breathing room read as a clinical system staff already trust. Restraint does the reassurance work; nothing shouts.

Direction 03 · Approachable

Warm Welcome

Human and welcoming without losing composure: a soft cream canvas, a floating rounded card, and a warm clay accent paired with a friendly serif. It lowers the temperature for non-technical staff — the door is secure, but it greets you. Warmth is grounded, never playful.

Direction 04 · Modern-secure

Secure Modern

A confident, current security product: a deep slate canvas, a single bright indigo accent, geometric type and mono detailing on codes. Darkness here signals focus and protection rather than mood — exact, modern, and clearly built to guard. The precision is the reassurance.
